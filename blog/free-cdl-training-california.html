Free CDL Training in California: Companies That Pay for Your CDL (2026)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

California has some of the highest CDL school costs in the country — which makes company-paid training programs especially valuable here. Carriers actively recruit in California because the state's massive freight market and consistent driver shortage mean they need new drivers constantly.

🌴 Important California Note

Company-paid CDL training in California operates the same as other states — carriers pay 100% of school costs for a 1-year commitment. However, California's AB5 law significantly affects owner-operator trucking. If you're considering going independent after your commitment year, research AB5 carefully before making that decision.

California-Specific Advantages for New CDL Drivers

  • Port drayage pays extremely well — After your training commitment, port drivers at Long Beach/LA earn $75,000–$100,000 as company drivers
  • Agricultural refrigerated freight is always in demand — The Central Valley produces a huge portion of US food, creating year-round reefer freight demand
  • California wages are among the highest in the US — Even with state income tax, experienced California drivers earn more than most other states

State Aid for California CDL Training

If company-paid programs don't work for your situation, California also offers:

  • California EDD workforce funding — Employment Development Department training grants for eligible workers
  • CalWORKs — CDL training funding for eligible CalWORKs participants
  • America's Job Center of California (AJCC) — WIOA funding for approved CDL programs
