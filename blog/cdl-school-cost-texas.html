How Much Does CDL School Cost in Texas? (2026 Real Prices)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Texas has more CDL schools than almost any other state — which creates competitive pricing and options at every budget level. Here's exactly what you'll pay for CDL training in Texas in 2026, from the cheapest community college programs to the premium private schools.

🤠 Texas CDL School Cost Summary

Private CDL schools in Texas: $4,000–$9,000. Community college programs: $2,500–$5,000. Company-paid programs: $0 upfront. State licensing fees: approximately $113 total.

Notable CDL Schools in Texas

  • Lone Star College (Houston) — Community college program, lower tuition, well-respected in the Houston market
  • Texas State Technical College (multiple locations) — State-funded CDL programs at competitive prices statewide
  • Truck Driver Institute (multiple TX locations) — Private school, can start with as little as $225 out of pocket with financial assistance
  • North Texas Truck Driver Training (DFW area) — Private school with flexible scheduling
  • Coastal Truck Driving School (Houston) — Focuses on Class A, strong job placement assistance

The Free Path — Company-Paid CDL Training in Texas

Major carriers with Texas training programs include Werner Enterprises, Swift Transportation, Schneider National, and CR England. All of these will pay 100% of your CDL school costs in exchange for a 1-year driving commitment. This is how the majority of new Texas CDL drivers get their license — at zero upfront cost.

See the full list of companies offering free CDL training in our company-paid CDL training guide.

Financial Aid Options for Texas CDL Students

  • Workforce Solutions (Texas Workforce Commission) — Texas offers CDL training funding through its workforce development programs for eligible applicants
  • WIOA Grants — Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act funding is available at many Texas community colleges
  • Veterans Benefits (GI Bill) — Eligible veterans can use GI Bill benefits for CDL training at approved institutions
  • Carrier Tuition Reimbursement — Many carriers reimburse $3,000–$8,000 in school costs after 6–12 months of employment

Is CDL School Worth the Cost in Texas?

The average CDL driver in Texas earns $58,400 per year. At $6,000 for school, your payback period is roughly 5–6 weeks of working. Even at the high end of $9,000, you recoup the cost within 2–3 months of employment. CDL school is one of the best return-on-investment credentials available — especially in Texas where freight demand is enormous and jobs are plentiful.

Bottom Line

CDL school in Texas costs $2,500–$9,000 depending on where you go — or $0 if you use a company-paid program. With licensing fees adding another $200–$325, total out-of-pocket costs for most Texas CDL students range from $2,700 to $9,325. The investment pays for itself within weeks of starting work.
