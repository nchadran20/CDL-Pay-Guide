What Is a CDL Learner's Permit (CLP)? How to Get One in 2026

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

The Commercial Learner's Permit (CLP) is the first official step toward getting your CDL. Before you can drive a commercial vehicle — even in training — you need a CLP in your wallet. Here's exactly what it is, how to get it, and what it allows you to do.

📋 CLP Fast Facts

What it is: A learner's permit allowing you to practice driving a commercial vehicle. Cost: $10–$50 depending on state. How to get it: Pass written tests at your state DMV. Minimum hold time before CDL test: 14 days (federal law). Valid for: 180 days in most states.

What the CLP Allows You to Do

With a CLP you can:

  • Drive a commercial vehicle on public roads for training purposes
  • Practice pre-trip inspections, backing, and road skills
  • Complete your ELDT (Entry Level Driver Training) behind-the-wheel requirements

With a CLP you cannot:

  • Drive alone — a CDL holder must be in the passenger seat at all times
  • Drive at night if your CLP has a night restriction (some states add this)
  • Transport passengers in a passenger-carrying vehicle
  • Operate a vehicle that requires a Hazmat endorsement
  • Take your CDL skills test until 14 days after getting your CLP

Written Tests Required for a Class A CLP

The number and type of tests required depends on what type of CDL you're getting. For a Class A CDL, you typically need to pass three written exams at the DMV:

TestWhat It CoversQuestions (typical)
General KnowledgeFederal trucking regulations, safety rules, vehicle inspection basics, shifting and backing, cargo handling50 questions
Combination VehiclesCoupling and uncoupling trailers, air brakes in combination vehicles, turning and backing combinations20 questions
Air BrakesAir brake system components, proper braking techniques, pre-trip inspection of air brakes25 questions

Additional tests are required if you want endorsements on your CLP (Hazmat, Passenger, School Bus, Tanker, Doubles/Triples). You can add these at the same DMV visit.

How to Prepare for the Written Tests

  • Your state's CDL manual — Available free online at your state's DMV website. Every question comes directly from this manual.
  • CDL practice test apps — Apps like CDL Prep, Zutobi, and DMV.org have hundreds of practice questions organized by test type. Most drivers study 1–2 weeks and pass on the first attempt.
  • YouTube — Search for CDL written test walkthroughs for your state. Many free study guides available.

Most people who fail the written test do so because they didn't study enough or focused on the wrong sections. Don't skip the air brakes test — it's the one drivers underestimate most.

The CDL CLP Process Step by Step

  1. Get your DOT physical done first — you need the medical certificate before the DMV will issue your CLP
  2. Study the CDL manual — give yourself 1–2 weeks
  3. Go to your state DMV with: regular driver's license, DOT medical certificate, proof of residency, and payment for fees
  4. Take and pass the written tests (General Knowledge, Combination, Air Brakes for Class A)
  5. Pay the CLP fee ($10–$50)
  6. Receive your CLP — now you can begin practicing with a CDL holder in the passenger seat
  7. Wait at least 14 days before scheduling your CDL skills test

CLP Validity Period

Most states issue a CLP valid for 180 days. If you don't complete your CDL skills test within that period, your CLP expires and you'll need to reapply. Plan your CDL school timeline so that you take your skills test well before the 180-day mark.
