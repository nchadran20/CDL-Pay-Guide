How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in California? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in California — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in California by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 California Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in California: $63,200/year. California has state income tax up to 13.3% which significantly reduces take-home pay compared to no-tax states. Major freight hubs in Los Angeles, Fresno, Sacramento, and Oakland. Port of LA is the busiest port in the US.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a California Truck Driver

  • Target port drayage in LA/Long Beach — Container drayage pays very well and keeps you home nightly.
  • Refrigerated freight from the Central Valley — California produces a huge portion of US food. Reefer drivers earn strong per-mile rates.
  • Hazmat pays a significant premium — California's industrial base creates strong demand for hazmat-endorsed drivers.
  • Research AB5 before going owner-operator — California's AB5 law has major implications for independent truckers.

CDL Requirements in California

To drive commercially in California you need a valid California CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within California) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid California driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
