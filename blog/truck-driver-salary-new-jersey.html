How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in New Jersey? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in New Jersey — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in New Jersey by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 New Jersey Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in New Jersey: $67,630/year. New Jersey has a graduated income tax ranging from 1.4% to 10.75% — among the higher state tax rates. However, raw CDL salaries are among the highest in the US. New Jersey is the number one state in the US for CDL driver pay. The Port of New York and New Jersey, the Amazon logistics network, and proximity to Philadelphia and NYC create massive freight demand.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a New Jersey Truck Driver

  • New Jersey has the highest average CDL salary in the US — The combination of port freight, NYC proximity, and dense retail distribution creates salary levels unmatched by any other state.
  • Port drayage at Port of NY/NJ can pay $80,000–$110,000 — Container drivers working the port are home nightly and earn premium rates due to complexity and demand.
  • Union jobs in NJ are very accessible — Teamsters positions at UPS, ABF, and other carriers are more available in NJ than most states, and union pay is significantly higher.
  • Pharmaceutical freight pays a premium — New Jersey's massive pharma industry (nicknamed the Medicine Cabinet of the World) creates specialized, high-paying freight opportunities.

CDL Requirements in New Jersey

To drive commercially in New Jersey you need a valid New Jersey CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within New Jersey) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid New Jersey driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
