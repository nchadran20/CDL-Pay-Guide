How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Florida? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Florida — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Florida by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Florida Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Florida: $54,662/year. Florida has no state income tax — your full paycheck stays in your pocket. Major freight corridors along I-95 and I-75. Port of Miami and Port Tampa are major import hubs. Year-round warm climate with no winter driving hazards.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Florida Truck Driver

  • Port freight in Miami and Jacksonville pays well — International trade creates steady drayage demand at premium rates.
  • Refrigerated freight is always in demand — Florida imports enormous amounts of produce and seafood requiring reefer transport.
  • No state income tax advantage — Florida's average salary looks lower than some states, but you keep significantly more of every paycheck.
  • Year-round driving advantage — No winter downtime means more miles annually than drivers in northern states.

CDL Requirements in Florida

To drive commercially in Florida you need a valid Florida CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Florida) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Florida driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
