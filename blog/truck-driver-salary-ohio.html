How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Ohio? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Ohio — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Ohio by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Ohio Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Ohio: $56,100/year. Ohio has a state income tax ranging from 2.765% to 3.99% — relatively moderate. Ohio sits at the crossroads of I-70, I-71, I-75, and I-80 — making it one of the most strategically located freight states in the US. Manufacturing is a major freight driver.

  • Columbus is becoming a top Midwest logistics hub — The city's central location and lower cost of living are attracting massive distribution investment. Driver demand is growing fast.
  • Automotive freight pays well — Ohio's auto manufacturing legacy creates steady specialized freight for car haulers and parts distributors.

  • Multiple interstate corridors mean consistent freight — Ohio drivers rarely face long waits for loads due to the state's freight crossroads location.

CDL Requirements in Ohio

To drive commercially in Ohio you need a valid Ohio CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Ohio) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Ohio driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
