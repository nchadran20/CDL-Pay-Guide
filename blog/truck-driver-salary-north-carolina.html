How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in North Carolina? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in North Carolina — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in North Carolina by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 North Carolina Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in North Carolina: $56,800/year. North Carolina has a flat income tax rate of 4.5% for 2026 — one of the lower rates in the Southeast. Charlotte is a major Southeast freight hub. The Port of Wilmington handles growing container volume. Major distribution centers from Amazon, Walmart, and food companies are expanding rapidly.

  • Charlotte is the fastest-growing freight market in the Carolinas — The city's explosive growth means constant new distribution centers and driver demand.

  • Port of Wilmington is expanding — Growing container volume is creating new drayage opportunities for local NC drivers.
  • Low flat income tax helps — North Carolina's 4.5% flat tax is competitive with neighboring states and keeps more of your earnings.

CDL Requirements in North Carolina

To drive commercially in North Carolina you need a valid North Carolina CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within North Carolina) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid North Carolina driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
