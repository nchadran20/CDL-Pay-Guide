Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Tennessee — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Tennessee by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Tennessee Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Tennessee: $54,200/year. Tennessee has no state income tax on wages — every dollar you earn stays in your pocket. Geographically centered in the eastern US — ideal for regional and OTR drivers. Nashville and Memphis are major freight hubs. Home to hundreds of distribution centers.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Tennessee Truck Driver

  • No state income tax is a significant advantage — A $54,000 salary in Tennessee effectively compares to $60,000+ in high-tax states like California or New York.
  • Memphis is a freight goldmine — FedEx World Hub processes millions of packages daily. Driver demand around Memphis is consistently high.
  • Nashville's growth creates strong local demand — The city's rapid population growth means constant need for local delivery and distribution drivers.
  • Central location maximizes regional driving — Tennessee sits within a day's drive of most major eastern US markets, making it ideal for regional routes.

CDL Requirements in Tennessee

To drive commercially in Tennessee you need a valid Tennessee CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Tennessee) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Tennessee driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
