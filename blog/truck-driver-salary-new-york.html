How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in New York? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in New York — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in New York by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 New York Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in New York: $65,800/year. New York has high state income tax up to 10.9%, plus NYC local tax for city drivers — significantly reducing take-home pay. One of the highest-paying states for raw CDL salary. Massive freight demand in the NYC metro. Port of NY/NJ is the busiest port on the East Coast.

Top Trucking Companies Hiring in New York

  • UPS Freight — Union (Teamsters) positions with excellent pay, benefits, and pension
  • XPO Logistics — Major LTL presence throughout New York
  • Old Dominion Freight — Regional LTL routes, consistent home time
  • FedEx Freight — Regional LTL throughout upstate and downstate NY
  • Wegmans Transportation — Upstate NY grocery chain with excellent culture and pay

How to Maximize Your Salary as a New York Truck Driver

  • Union jobs pay 20–40% more in New York — Teamsters positions at UPS, ABF, and others include pension benefits unavailable at non-union carriers.
  • NYC metro local driving pays extremely well — The complexity of city driving commands a premium. Local NYC drivers often out-earn OTR drivers.
  • Port drayage is a hidden gem — Container drivers at the Port of NY/NJ earn $75,000–$100,000 and are home nightly.
  • Upstate NY offers great value — Strong salaries in Buffalo or Albany with dramatically lower cost of living than NYC.

CDL Requirements in New York

To drive commercially in New York you need a valid New York CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within New York) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid New York driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
