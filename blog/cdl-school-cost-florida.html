How Much Does CDL School Cost in Florida? (2026 Real Prices)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Florida is one of the most affordable states for CDL training with competitive pricing across private schools and some of the most accessible community college programs in the Southeast. Here's the complete breakdown of what you'll pay in 2026.

🌴 Florida CDL School Cost Summary

Private CDL schools: $3,500–$8,500. Community college programs: $2,000–$4,500. Company-paid programs: $0 upfront. Florida state fees: approximately $100–$200 total.

Financial Aid for Florida CDL Students

  • CareerSource Florida — Florida's workforce development agency often funds CDL training for eligible applicants — check your local CareerSource office
  • Bright Futures / Florida Student Assistance Grants — Some eligible CDL programs at Florida community colleges qualify
  • WIOA Title I funds — Available through CareerSource Florida career centers
  • Florida GI Bill (Vocational Rehabilitation) — Veterans can access funding for CDL training through state veteran's services
  • Carrier Tuition Reimbursement — Many carriers hiring in Florida reimburse $3,000–$6,000 in school costs after employment

Notable CDL Schools in Florida

  • Florida Gateway College (Lake City) — Community college with CDL program, competitive pricing
  • Withlacoochee Technical College (Inverness) — Affordable CDL training in Central Florida
  • Atlantic Coast Truck Driving School (Jacksonville) — Private school with strong job placement
  • South Florida Truck Driver Training (Miami area) — Serves the large South Florida freight market

Is CDL School Worth It in Florida?

Florida CDL drivers earn an average of $54,662/year — plus no state income tax means you keep every dollar. At $5,000 for school, payback takes under 7 weeks of working. Florida's year-round warm weather and consistent freight demand make it a smart investment for CDL school.
