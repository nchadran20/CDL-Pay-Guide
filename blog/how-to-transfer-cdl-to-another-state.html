How to Transfer Your CDL to Another State (2026 Step-by-Step Guide)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

If you've moved to a new state with your CDL, you're required by federal law to transfer it to your new state within 30 days. The process is straightforward and you do NOT need to retake written or skills tests — but you do need to bring the right documents. Here's exactly how to do it.

📋 CDL Transfer Fast Facts

Deadline: 30 days after establishing residency in new state. Written tests required: No. Skills test required: No. Your driving record: Transfers with you. Violations from old state: Follow you permanently. Cost: Standard CDL fee in new state ($30–$100).

The 30-Day Rule

Federal regulations require CDL holders to obtain a CDL from their new state of domicile within 30 days of establishing residency. You can only hold a CDL from one state — you cannot have CDLs from two states simultaneously. Once you get your new state CDL, your old state CDL is surrendered and cancelled.

Step-by-Step CDL Transfer Process

  1. Establish residency in the new state — Get a lease agreement, utility bill, or other proof of your new address. Some states require proof before accepting your application.
  2. Gather your documents:
    • Your current valid CDL from your old state
    • Proof of new state residency (utility bill, lease, bank statement)
    • Social Security card or documentation
    • Current DOT medical certificate
    • Payment for the new state CDL fee
  3. Go to your new state's DMV — No appointment needed at most locations but check your new state's DMV website to confirm.
  4. Pass a vision test — Most states require a basic vision screening (reading an eye chart). This is not a full DOT physical — just a quick vision check.
  5. Surrender your old CDL — The DMV will take your old CDL and send notification to the old state to cancel it.
  6. Pay the fee — Standard CDL license fee for the new state, typically $30–$100.
  7. Receive your new CDL — Some states issue a temporary paper license immediately and mail the permanent plastic card within 1–3 weeks.

Can Violations from Another State Be Hidden by Transferring?

No — this is a common misconception. The CDL Driver Information System (CDLIS) is a national database that tracks all CDL holder records across states. When you transfer your CDL, your complete violation history transfers with you. There is no way to "reset" your driving record by moving states. Violations, suspensions, and disqualifications follow your CDL license regardless of which state it's issued in.

Notifying Your Employer

Federal regulations also require you to notify your employer within 30 days of any address change. Your employer needs your current state of domicile for their records and insurance purposes. Don't forget this step — it's required by law for CDL holders.
