How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Michigan? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Michigan — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Michigan by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Michigan Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Michigan: $59,400/year. Michigan has a flat state income tax rate of 4.25%. Michigan is the heart of the US automotive industry. Car hauling and auto parts freight create unique high-paying CDL opportunities not found in most other states.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Michigan Truck Driver

  • Auto hauling pays the highest in Michigan — Car haulers transporting new vehicles from Detroit-area plants earn $70,000–$95,000 annually — among the best CDL pay in the state.
  • Auto parts just-in-time delivery is always in demand — Auto manufacturers require constant parts delivery on tight schedules. Drivers who specialize in this earn premium rates.
  • Canadian border crossings create opportunities — Michigan shares the largest trade crossing with Canada. Drivers comfortable with border procedures are in high demand.
  • Winter driving expertise has real value — Michigan winters are serious. Drivers with clean records in difficult winter conditions command higher rates from cautious carriers.

CDL Requirements in Michigan

To drive commercially in Michigan you need a valid Michigan CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Michigan) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Michigan driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
