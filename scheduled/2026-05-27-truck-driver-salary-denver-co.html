Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

Here's exactly what CDL truck drivers earn in Denver in 2026 — real salary data by experience level, driving type, and the top employers actively hiring in the area.

📊 Denver Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Denver: $65,000/year. Denver is the largest freight hub between the Midwest and West Coast. The city's mountain location creates demand for specialized drivers comfortable with I-70 mountain passes. Oil and gas freight from the DJ Basin pays among the highest CDL rates in the state.

Top Employers Hiring in Denver

  • Amazon — Large Denver-area fulfillment operation with local and linehaul positions
  • Walmart — Distribution centers in multiple Denver-area locations
  • Halliburton / SLB — Oil field support freight from Denver to DJ Basin and Piceance Basin — top-paying CDL work in Colorado
  • Werner Enterprises — Strong Intermountain West regional routes through Denver
  • King Soopers / Kroger — Colorado grocery chain distribution, local routes available
  • Old Dominion Freight — Regional LTL with consistent Denver area work

How to Maximize Your Pay in Denver

  • Mountain driving expertise pays a premium — Drivers certified and experienced with I-70 through the Rockies earn more than flatland drivers in Denver — carriers value this skill
  • DJ Basin oil field freight is the highest-paying CDL work near Denver — Hazmat and tanker drivers supporting the Weld County oil fields north of Denver earn $80,000–$110,000 annually
  • Colorado's 4.4% flat income tax is competitive — Not zero like Texas or Tennessee, but significantly lower than California or New York
  • Denver is growing fast — Population growth and Amazon expansion are driving consistent increases in driver demand and wages year over year
