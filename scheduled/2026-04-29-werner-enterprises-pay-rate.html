Werner Enterprises Pay Rate 2026: What Drivers Actually Earn

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Werner Enterprises is one of the largest truckload carriers in the US and one of the most common entry points for new CDL drivers. Here's the honest breakdown of what Werner actually pays in 2026 — including the numbers they advertise and what drivers report in practice.

Werner 2026 Pay Summary

Starting pay: $0.54–$0.60/mile. Average first-year earnings: $55,000–$65,000. Sign-on bonus: up to $10,000 for experienced drivers. Company-paid CDL training available for new drivers.

Werner Benefits Package (2026)

  • Medical, dental, and vision insurance (available after 30 days)
  • 401(k) with company match
  • Paid vacation after 1 year
  • Paid orientation
  • New trucks with APUs (no idling)
  • Pet and rider policies available
  • Weekly direct deposit

Werner Sign-On Bonuses (2026)

  • Experienced CDL-A drivers: up to $10,000 sign-on bonus (paid in installments over 12 months)
  • New drivers through Werner's training program: bonus paid after completing training commitment
  • Team driver bonuses: additional incentives for team positions

Werner Paid Training Program

Werner offers company-paid CDL training for new drivers. Werner pays 100% of tuition in exchange for a 1-year driving commitment. New drivers start earning during orientation and team-driving phase. This is how the majority of Werner's new driver hires enter the company.

What Werner Drivers Actually Say

Werner consistently earns mixed reviews. The positives drivers mention most often: consistent freight, newer equipment, and accessible training for new drivers. The most common complaints: end-of-month dispatch pressure, inconsistent miles in slower freight periods, and the typical OTR lifestyle challenges of being away from home.

Werner ranks as a solid first-carrier choice for new CDL drivers — not necessarily where you'll spend your whole career, but a reliable launchpad.
