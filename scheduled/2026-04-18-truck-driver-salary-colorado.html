How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Colorado? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Colorado — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Colorado by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Colorado Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Colorado: $61,800/year. Colorado has a flat state income tax rate of 4.4%. Denver is the largest freight hub between the Midwest and the West Coast. Colorado's mountain corridors require specialized driving skills that command premium pay. Oil and gas freight in the DJ Basin pays top dollar.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Colorado Truck Driver

  • Mountain driving expertise commands a premium — Drivers comfortable with I-70 through the Rockies, chain laws, and steep grades are in high demand and earn more than flatland drivers.
  • Oil and gas freight in the DJ Basin pays very well — The Weld County area north of Denver is one of the most productive oil fields in the US. Hazmat and tanker drivers there earn $80,000–$110,000 annually.
  • Denver is the gateway to the West — As a crossroads between the Midwest and West Coast, Denver has consistent OTR freight availability in both directions.
  • Winter driving skills are essential and valuable — Colorado mountain passes close frequently in winter. Drivers with proven mountain winter records are prioritized by carriers.

CDL Requirements in Colorado

To drive commercially in Colorado you need a valid Colorado CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Colorado) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Colorado driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
