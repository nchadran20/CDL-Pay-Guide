Best Trucking Companies for Flatbed Drivers in 2026

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

Flatbed driving pays more than dry van — but the extra money comes with extra work. Tarping, securing loads, and managing oversized freight requires skill and physical effort that not every driver wants. For those who do, flatbed is one of the most financially rewarding niches in trucking. Here's where to go for the best flatbed CDL jobs in 2026.

Why Flatbed Pays More

Flatbed drivers earn $5,000–$15,000 more per year than comparable dry van drivers because the work is harder — loads must be secured with chains, straps, and binders, and many loads require tarping. The skill premium and physical demands justify the pay difference. Add oversized load work and that premium climbs further.

Top Flatbed Carriers in 2026

1. TMC Transportation — Best Overall Flatbed Carrier

TMC is widely considered the gold standard in flatbed trucking. They specialize exclusively in flatbed freight, run Peterbilt equipment, and offer one of the best flatbed training programs in the industry. Pay is above average, freight is consistent, and the company culture is driver-focused. Requires some experience — not ideal for brand-new CDL holders.

  • Pay: $72,000–$92,000/year
  • Freight: Steel, lumber, machinery, building materials
  • Equipment: Peterbilt 579 — newer fleet
  • Training: Paid apprenticeship program for flatbed skills
  • Best for: Drivers serious about a flatbed career long-term

2. Melton Truck Lines — Best for New-to-Flatbed Drivers

Melton offers one of the best flatbed training programs for drivers transitioning from dry van. They'll teach you how to tarp and secure loads properly — a valuable skill set. Pay is competitive and the company has a strong reputation for consistent flatbed freight.

  • Pay: $65,000–$85,000/year
  • Training: Yes — flatbed-specific training available
  • Best for: Dry van drivers wanting to transition to flatbed for better pay

3. Maverick Transportation — Best Automated Tarping Technology

Maverick has invested heavily in automated tarping systems that reduce the physical burden of flatbed work. For drivers who want the flatbed pay premium without as much manual tarping labor, Maverick's system is a genuine differentiator. Strong freight network throughout the Southeast and Midwest.

  • Pay: $68,000–$85,000/year
  • Specialty: Automated tarping system reduces physical demand
  • Best for: Drivers who want flatbed pay without maximum physical demand

4. J.B. Hunt Flatbed — National Scale with Flatbed Specialty

J.B. Hunt's flatbed division offers the financial stability of a large public company with dedicated flatbed freight. Good for drivers who want name-brand stability combined with the flatbed pay premium.

  • Pay: $70,000–$88,000/year
  • Best for: Drivers who want a large carrier with flatbed specialization

5. RJ Corman Railroad Group — Best for Specialized Flatbed

RJ Corman specializes in transporting railroad equipment, construction materials, and specialized freight that requires flatbed expertise. Niche work pays premium rates and experienced flatbed drivers are in high demand here.

  • Pay: $75,000–$95,000/year for experienced drivers
  • Best for: Experienced flatbed drivers wanting specialized, higher-paying work
