Can You Get a CDL with a DUI on Your Record? (2026 Honest Answer)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

This is one of the most searched CDL questions — and the answer is more nuanced than a simple yes or no. Whether a DUI prevents you from getting a CDL depends on when it happened, how many you have, and what state you're in. Here's the straight answer.

⚠️ The Bottom Line

A single DUI more than 10 years ago generally does not prevent you from getting a CDL in most states. A DUI within the past 10 years disqualifies you from operating a commercial vehicle under federal law. A second DUI at any point results in a lifetime CDL disqualification at most carriers.

Federal CDL Disqualification Rules for DUI

Under federal FMCSA regulations, the following DUI-related convictions result in CDL disqualification:

OffenseDisqualification PeriodNotes
First DUI/DWI conviction1 year from convictionIf transporting hazmat, disqualification is 3 years
Second DUI/DWI convictionLifetime (federal)No CDL ever again under federal rules
DUI in a commercial vehicle1 year minimumApplies even if you had a regular license at the time
Refusal to take BAC testSame as DUI convictionTreated identically to a DUI conviction
BAC of .04 or higher in a CMV1 year minimumNote: the CDL limit is .04%, not .08% like regular vehicles

The 10-Year Lookback Period

While the federal disqualification period for a first DUI is just 1 year, most carriers use a 10-year MVR lookback period when hiring. This means:

  • Even after the federal disqualification ends, most major carriers won't hire you with a DUI in the past 10 years
  • Some carriers have a 5-year lookback — check with specific carriers about their policy
  • After 10 years, a single past DUI generally does not prevent hiring at most carriers

What About Getting the CDL Itself?

The state DMV will issue you a CDL after your disqualification period ends — the CDL itself is not permanently revoked for a single DUI unless you had the DUI in a commercial vehicle or it's your second offense. The bigger barrier is carrier hiring policies, not the CDL issuance itself.

The CDL Lower BAC Standard

Commercial drivers are held to a stricter BAC standard than regular drivers:

  • Regular vehicle: 0.08% BAC — legally drunk
  • Commercial vehicle: 0.04% BAC — disqualifying for CDL holders

This means CDL holders effectively cannot have even 1–2 drinks before driving a commercial vehicle. The standard applies even if you were in your personal vehicle when you got a DUI — if you hold a CDL, your CDL can be affected by a DUI in any vehicle.

DUI in a Personal Vehicle vs Commercial Vehicle

A DUI received while driving your personal car (not a commercial vehicle) can still affect your CDL. States are required to notify the FMCSA of all DUI convictions for CDL holders, regardless of which vehicle was involved. The disqualification applies to your CDL even if the arrest was in your personal car.

Options If You Have a Recent DUI

  • Wait out the disqualification period — If it's been less than a year, you must wait. Use the time to get your CDL training completed so you're ready to drive the moment the disqualification ends.
  • Target smaller regional carriers — Some smaller carriers have more flexible hiring policies than national carriers. They may hire with a DUI that's 3–5 years old where Werner or Swift wouldn't.
  • Be upfront in applications — Lying about a DUI on a carrier application is worse than the DUI itself. Background checks always surface it.
  • Consider intrastate-only work — Some state regulations and smaller operations are less strict than federal rules for interstate work.
