Free CDL Training in Texas: Companies That Pay for Your CDL (2026)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

You do not need to pay for CDL school in Texas. Multiple major carriers operate company-paid training programs throughout the state — paying 100% of your tuition in exchange for a 1-year driving commitment. This is how the majority of new Texas CDL drivers enter the industry.

🤠 The Honest Deal

Company-paid CDL training is genuinely free — no money down, no hidden fees. The catch is a 1-year work commitment. Leave early and you may owe back a prorated portion of the tuition (typically $3,000–$6,000). Complete the year and you owe nothing. Your CDL is yours to keep forever.

How the Application Process Works

  1. Apply online — Go to the carrier's website and apply for their CDL training program. Most have a simple online application.
  2. Pre-employment screening — Background check, MVR check, drug test. Clean record required — no DUIs in past 10 years.
  3. Get your CLP first — Most carriers require you to have your Commercial Learner's Permit before starting. You handle this yourself at the Texas DPS office.
  4. Attend their CDL school — Typically 3–4 weeks of training paid for entirely by the carrier.
  5. Carrier orientation — 1–2 weeks of company-specific training before you drive solo.
  6. Drive with a trainer — Most carriers pair you with an experienced driver for 3–6 weeks before you go solo.

What Disqualifies You from Company-Paid Programs

  • DUI or DWI conviction in the past 10 years
  • Multiple serious traffic violations (reckless driving, excessive speeding)
  • Failed DOT drug test on record
  • Certain felony convictions (varies by carrier)
  • Unable to pass DOT physical examination

Texas-Specific Advantage

Texas is one of the most favorable states for company-paid CDL training because:

  • Major carriers have large Texas operations and actively need drivers here
  • Texas allows third-party skills testing — many carrier training programs handle your skills test directly, saving time
  • Texas has no state income tax, so your first-year earnings go further
  • The Texas freight market is enormous — you'll rarely sit without a load
