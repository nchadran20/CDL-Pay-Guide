Truck Driver Salary in Atlanta, GA: (2026 Real Numbers)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're a CDL driver in Atlanta or considering a trucking career in the area, this guide gives you the real numbers. Here's exactly what truck drivers earn in Atlanta in 2026 — by experience level, driving type, and which employers are paying the most.

📊 Atlanta Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Atlanta: $62,000/year. Atlanta is the freight capital of the Southeast. Hartsfield-Jackson Airport drives enormous air cargo ground freight, and the city sits at the intersection of I-75, I-85, and I-20 — three of the most important freight corridors in the eastern US.

Top Employers Hiring CDL Drivers in Atlanta

  • UPS — Massive Atlanta hub operations across all service levels — one of the largest CDL employers in the city
  • Home Depot — Headquartered in Atlanta, runs one of the largest private fleets in the country with above-market pay
  • Coca-Cola — Distribution throughout the Atlanta metro, local routes with daily home time
  • Amazon — Large Atlanta fulfillment network with local and linehaul positions
  • XPO Logistics — Strong LTL presence in Atlanta, consistent freight
  • Southeastern Freight Lines — Atlanta-area regional LTL carrier with solid pay and culture

How to Maximize Your Pay in Atlanta

  • UPS Atlanta operations pay union wages — Teamsters positions at the UPS Atlanta hub pay 20–30% above non-union equivalents with pension benefits
  • Airport freight pays a premium — Hartsfield-Jackson ground support and air cargo drivers earn above standard rates
  • Private fleet jobs with Atlanta-headquartered companies — Home Depot, Coca-Cola, and similar corporate fleets consistently pay above market rates
  • Target south metro corridors — Logistics parks in McDonough, Palmetto, and Douglasville have strong demand with slightly less congestion than intown Atlanta
