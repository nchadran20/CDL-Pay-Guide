How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Illinois? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Illinois — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Illinois by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Illinois Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Illinois: $61,500/year. Illinois has a flat state income tax rate of 4.95%. Chicago is one of the three largest freight markets in the US. Illinois sits at the center of the national rail and highway network, making it a critical freight state.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Illinois Truck Driver

  • Chicago intermodal pays very well — Moving containers between the massive Chicago rail yards and warehouses is in constant demand and pays above average.
  • The Joliet corridor is booming — Massive new warehouses along I-55 south of Chicago are creating huge demand for local and regional drivers.
  • Avoid Chicago traffic by targeting suburban distribution — Many drivers earn as much as city drivers while avoiding the brutal Chicago traffic by working the suburban warehouse corridor.
  • Hazmat and tanker pay significant premiums — Illinois industrial freight creates strong demand for endorsed drivers.

CDL Requirements in Illinois

To drive commercially in Illinois you need a valid Illinois CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Illinois) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Illinois driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
