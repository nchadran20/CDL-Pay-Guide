Free CDL Training in Florida: Companies That Pay for Your CDL (2026)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Florida has one of the most active company-paid CDL training markets in the country. The state's year-round warm climate, massive freight corridors along I-95 and I-75, and two major ports create constant demand for new drivers — and the carriers hiring here are competing hard for talent by offering free training.

🌴 Bottom Line Upfront

Multiple major carriers will pay 100% of your CDL school costs in Florida. You commit to driving for them for 1 year. Leave early and you may owe back a prorated tuition amount. Complete the year and you owe nothing — and your CDL is yours forever.

Florida-Specific Programs Worth Knowing

  • Publix Transportation — Florida's largest grocery chain sometimes accepts drivers after a short experience period. After your 1-year commitment elsewhere, Publix is one of the best local fleet jobs in Florida.
  • Port of Miami / Jacksonville drayage carriers — Some port drayage companies offer on-the-job training for local CDL drivers. Not a traditional school program, but worth researching if you want to stay in South Florida.
  • CareerSource Florida — Florida's state workforce system can sometimes fund CDL school at approved institutions if company-paid programs don't work out for you.

What to Expect During Company Training in Florida

  1. Apply online and pass pre-employment screening (background, MVR, drug test)
  2. Get your CLP at a Florida DHSMV office — required before most programs start
  3. Attend 3–4 weeks of CDL school paid by the carrier
  4. Complete 1–2 weeks of company orientation
  5. Drive with a trainer for 4–6 weeks before going solo

Florida Advantage for New Drivers

Florida's no state income tax means your training-period pay — though lower — goes further than in most states. The year-round weather means no seasonal slowdowns in freight, and Florida's port freight creates strong local job options once your commitment year is complete.
