How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Texas? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Texas is one of the best states in the country for truck drivers. It has the largest highway network in the US, massive freight demand from oil and gas, agriculture, and international trade, and zero state income tax — meaning your paycheck goes further here than in most other states. Here's exactly what CDL drivers earn in Texas in 2026.

🤠 Texas Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Texas: $58,400/year. No state income tax. Over 200,000 CDL jobs available statewide. Major freight hubs in Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and El Paso.

Why Texas Is One of the Best States for CDL Drivers

Several factors make Texas stand out as a top state for truck drivers:

  • No state income tax — A driver earning $65,000 in Texas takes home approximately $5,000–$6,000 more per year than the same driver in California or New York after state taxes.
  • Massive freight volume — Texas moves more freight than any other state. Oil and gas, agriculture, manufacturing, and international border trade all create constant demand for CDL drivers.
  • Low cost of living — Outside of Austin, Texas has a low cost of living relative to pay. Your salary goes further here than in high-pay coastal states.
  • Job availability — Texas consistently ranks in the top 3 states for total CDL job openings. Finding work here is rarely a problem.
  • Growing market — Texas added more people than any other state over the past decade. More people means more freight, which means more driver demand.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Texas Truck Driver

The drivers earning $75,000+ in Texas share a few common strategies:

  • Get your Hazmat endorsement — Oil and gas freight in Texas pays significantly more than standard dry van. Hazmat + Tanker (the X endorsement) is the most valuable combination in the Texas market.
  • Target the Permian Basin — West Texas oil field driving is physically demanding and keeps you away from home but it's among the highest-paying CDL work in the country at $80,000–$110,000 annually.
  • Run border freight through Laredo — International trade routes pay premium rates due to complexity and demand.
  • Build experience then go dedicated — After 1–2 years of OTR experience, dedicated accounts with major shippers in Texas (Amazon, Walmart, HEB) offer excellent pay with better home time.

CDL Requirements in Texas

To drive commercially in Texas you need a Texas CDL issued by the Texas DPS (Department of Public Safety). Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Texas) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving
  • Valid Texas driver's license
  • DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written and skills tests
  • CDL license fee: approximately $97 plus $16 CLP fee

Bottom Line

Texas truck drivers earn a solid $58,400 on average in 2026 — and with no state income tax, that money goes further than in most competing states. The freight market is enormous, jobs are plentiful, and specialized work in oil and gas can push your income well above $80,000. If you're considering a CDL career or looking to relocate for better trucking pay, Texas is consistently one of the top states to consider.
