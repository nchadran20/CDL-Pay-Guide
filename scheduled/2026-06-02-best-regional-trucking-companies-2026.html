Best Regional Trucking Companies in 2026 (Ranked by Pay & Home Time)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

Regional driving is the sweet spot most experienced drivers settle into — better pay than local, better home time than OTR. But not all regional carriers deliver equally on both. Here's the honest ranking of the best regional trucking companies in 2026.

What "Regional" Actually Means

Regional driving covers a specific geographic zone — typically 4–5 states within a 1,000-mile radius. Most regional drivers are home on weekends and some nights during the week. Pay is between OTR (higher) and local (lower). See our full OTR vs Regional vs Local comparison for the complete lifestyle breakdown.

Top Regional Carriers in 2026

1. Old Dominion Freight Line — Best Overall Regional

Old Dominion's regional LTL network gives drivers consistent routes within defined geographic areas. Linehaul drivers run between terminals nightly and are home multiple times per week. Pay is above average for regional work, the company is financially strong, and driver satisfaction ratings are consistently the highest among major LTL carriers.

  • Pay: $68,000–$85,000/year
  • Home time: 3–5 nights home per week
  • Freight: LTL — multiple stops
  • Best for: Drivers who want stability and above-average regional pay

2. Estes Express Lines — Strong Southeast and Mid-Atlantic Regional

Estes is a privately held LTL carrier with a strong regional presence in the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic. Driver satisfaction is high — the private company structure means less pressure for Wall Street quarterly results and more focus on long-term driver relationships.

  • Pay: $65,000–$80,000/year
  • Home time: Frequent — LTL linehaul structure
  • Best for: Southeast and Mid-Atlantic drivers wanting LTL regional work

3. Averitt Express — Best Southeastern Regional

Averitt is a Nashville-based carrier with an excellent reputation in the Southeast. Consistently earns high marks for company culture and driver treatment. Regional routes throughout the Southeast with strong home time and competitive pay.

  • Pay: $62,000–$78,000/year
  • Home time: Most weekends, many weeknights
  • Best for: Southeast drivers who want strong culture and regional lifestyle

4. Werner Regional Division — Reliable National Regional Option

Werner's regional division covers multiple geographic zones nationwide. Not the highest pay among regional options but reliable home time and consistent freight. Good stepping stone from OTR to true local work.

  • Pay: $62,000–$76,000/year
  • Home time: Most weekends
  • Best for: Drivers transitioning from OTR wanting a national carrier with regional options

5. Melton Truck Lines — Best Flatbed Regional

Melton specializes in flatbed freight throughout the Central and Eastern US. Flatbed regional work pays more than standard dry van regional due to the added skills required. Strong company culture and above-average pay for regional flatbed work.

  • Pay: $65,000–$82,000/year
  • Home time: Weekly on most accounts
  • Best for: Flatbed-experienced drivers wanting regional lifestyle

How to Transition from OTR to Regional

Most regional positions require 6–12 months of OTR experience minimum. The standard path:

  1. Start OTR at a large carrier through their free training program
  2. Run hard for 12 months — build your experience and clean record
  3. Apply to regional carriers at the 12-month mark — you'll have multiple options
  4. Negotiate your starting rate based on your OTR experience — don't accept the entry-level rate if you have a year of clean driving
