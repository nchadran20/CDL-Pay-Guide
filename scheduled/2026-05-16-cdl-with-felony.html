Can You Get a CDL with a Felony? (2026 Complete Answer by Offense Type)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

A felony on your record does not automatically prevent you from getting a CDL — but certain specific felonies do, and most carriers have their own hiring policies that go beyond the federal minimum rules. Here's the complete breakdown by offense type.

⚠️ The Core Rule

There is no blanket federal rule preventing all felons from getting a CDL. Certain specific serious offenses result in permanent disqualification. For everything else, it depends on the offense type, how long ago it was, whether you're applying for a Hazmat endorsement, and which carrier you're applying to.

Felonies That Permanently Disqualify You from a CDL

Under federal FMCSA regulations, the following felony convictions result in lifetime CDL disqualification:

  • Using a commercial vehicle to commit a felony involving a controlled substance
  • Manslaughter using a commercial vehicle
  • Fleeing the scene of an accident involving a commercial vehicle
  • Causing a fatality through negligent operation of a commercial vehicle
  • Using a commercial vehicle in the commission of a felony involving human trafficking

Note the pattern: most permanent disqualifications involve the commercial vehicle itself being used in the crime. Felonies committed outside of commercial driving are treated differently.

The Hazmat Endorsement — Stricter Rules

If you want to add a Hazmat endorsement to your CDL, additional felony disqualifications apply through the TSA background check process. The following can prevent a Hazmat endorsement (but not a standard CDL):

  • Felony convictions involving explosives, firearms, or weapons
  • Violent felony convictions within the past 7 years
  • Certain felony drug convictions
  • Immigration violations
  • Connection to terrorism or terrorist organizations

You can have a standard CDL and be unable to get a Hazmat endorsement — these are two separate processes with different standards.

Carrier Hiring Policies — The Bigger Barrier

In practice, most felons find that getting the CDL itself is easier than getting hired by a carrier. Major national carriers like Werner, Swift, and Schneider run comprehensive background checks and typically will not hire drivers with:

  • Any violent felony in the past 5–10 years
  • Drug trafficking convictions
  • Theft or fraud convictions (particularly if large amounts were involved)
  • Sexual offense convictions

Where Drivers with Felony Records Have the Best Chances

  • Smaller regional carriers — More flexibility in hiring policies, often review cases individually
  • Owner-operator path — If you can get your own authority, you're not subject to carrier hiring policies
  • Intrastate/local work — Some state-level operations are less strict than interstate carriers
  • Time is your biggest asset — The further in the past an offense is, the better your chances with most carriers
