Truck Driver Salary in Chicago, IL: (2026 Real Numbers)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're a CDL driver in Chicago or considering a trucking career in the area, this guide gives you the real numbers. Here's exactly what truck drivers earn in Chicago in 2026 — by experience level, driving type, and which employers are paying the most.

📊 Chicago Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Chicago: $70,000/year. Chicago is the third largest freight market in the US. The city sits at the center of the national rail network and is a major hub for intermodal freight. High driver demand keeps wages well above national averages.

Top Employers Hiring CDL Drivers in Chicago

  • J.B. Hunt — Chicago intermodal operation is one of the largest in the country — strong pay and consistent work
  • UPS — Major Chicago hub with Teamsters union positions offering top-tier pay and benefits
  • Amazon — Extensive Chicago-area fulfillment network, one of the largest CDL employers in the market
  • XPO Logistics — Large Chicago LTL hub with consistent regional work
  • US Foods — Major Chicago food distribution operation with local routes
  • Walmart — Multiple Chicago-area distribution centers with dedicated routes

How to Maximize Your Pay in Chicago

  • Intermodal driving pays well in Chicago — J.B. Hunt and other intermodal carriers move containers between Chicago's massive rail yards and area warehouses. Drivers earn strong pay while staying local.
  • Union jobs pay 25–40% more — Teamsters positions at UPS, ABF Freight, and other unionized carriers are more accessible in Chicago than almost anywhere else in the country
  • Target Joliet and the I-55 warehouse corridor — Massive warehouse development south of Chicago offers strong CDL pay with less urban congestion than the city core
  • Night shift premium — Many Chicago linehaul and distribution positions pay a shift differential for overnight work — adding $3,000–$6,000 annually to base rates
