Schneider National Starting Pay 2026: Salary, Benefits & Training

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Schneider National stands out from most large carriers by offering multiple driving divisions — OTR, regional, intermodal, and dedicated — all within the same company. This means more career flexibility and the ability to shift your lifestyle without changing employers. Here's what Schneider pays in 2026.

Schneider 2026 Pay Summary

Average first-year earnings: $55,000–$70,000. Intermodal division (best home time): $58,000–$72,000. OTR division (highest pay): $65,000–$80,000. Sign-on bonus: up to $12,000 for experienced drivers.

Schneider's Free CDL Training Program

Schneider runs one of the most established company-paid CDL training programs in the industry. Key details:

  • 100% company-paid tuition — no money down
  • Training locations in 10+ states
  • 1-year driving commitment after graduation
  • Paid orientation and training allowance
  • Direct entry into Schneider's driving divisions upon graduation

Schneider Benefits

  • Medical, dental, vision insurance
  • 401(k) with 4% company match
  • Paid vacation and holidays
  • Profit sharing program
  • Employee stock purchase plan
  • Pet and rider policies

What Separates Schneider from Other Carriers

The intermodal division is Schneider's most unique offering — drivers haul containers between rail yards and warehouses on local routes, going home daily or near-daily. It pays slightly less than OTR but the quality-of-life improvement is significant. Many drivers start OTR at Schneider and transition to intermodal after gaining experience.

Schneider is publicly traded (NYSE: SNDR) and consistently profitable, which provides job stability above what smaller carriers can offer.
