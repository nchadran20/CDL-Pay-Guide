What Is a DOT Physical? What to Expect and How to Pass (2026)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

Before you can get your CDL, you need a DOT medical certificate from a certified examiner. This is non-negotiable — no CDL without it. Here's exactly what happens at a DOT physical, what conditions can disqualify you, and how to find a certified examiner in your area.

📋 DOT Physical Fast Facts

Cost: $50–$150. Duration: 30–60 minutes. Valid for: Up to 2 years (shorter if certain conditions are present). Where to go: FMCSA National Registry at nationalregistry.fmcsa.dot.gov. Pass rate: High for healthy adults.

What Is a DOT Physical?

A DOT physical is a medical examination required by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) for all commercial driver's license holders. It verifies that you are physically capable of safely operating a commercial motor vehicle. The exam is conducted by a medical professional on the FMCSA's National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners — not just any doctor will do.

What Can Disqualify You from a DOT Physical

These conditions are typically disqualifying unless waived by the FMCSA:

  • Epilepsy or seizure disorder (controlled or not)
  • Vision worse than 20/40 in either eye that cannot be corrected to standard
  • Current use of certain medications including methadone, Schedule I drugs, or amphetamines not prescribed
  • Insulin-dependent diabetes (special skill program available with additional process)
  • Severe cardiovascular conditions — certain heart conditions, recent heart surgery
  • Pacemaker (case-by-case basis)
  • Respiratory dysfunction that significantly limits oxygen levels

Blood Pressure — The Most Common Issue

High blood pressure is the most common reason drivers receive a shortened medical certificate or are temporarily disqualified. Here's how it works:

  • Below 140/90: Full 2-year certificate issued
  • 140–159/90–99 (Stage 1 hypertension): 1-year certificate — must recheck annually
  • 160–179/100–109 (Stage 2): 3-month temporary certificate — must get treated and recheck
  • 180+/110+ (Stage 3): Disqualified until blood pressure is controlled with treatment

If your blood pressure is borderline, avoid caffeine and strenuous activity the morning of your exam. If you take blood pressure medication, take it as prescribed — do not skip doses before your appointment.

How to Find a Certified DOT Examiner

You must use a medical examiner listed on the FMCSA's National Registry. Visit nationalregistry.fmcsa.dot.gov and search by zip code. Certified examiners include doctors, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, chiropractors, and other licensed medical professionals who have completed FMCSA training.

Cost ranges from $50–$150 depending on location. Urgent care clinics, occupational health clinics, and some truck stops have certified examiners on-site.

How Long Is a DOT Medical Certificate Valid?

A standard DOT medical certificate is valid for 2 years. It can be shorter if the examiner finds a condition requiring more frequent monitoring — typically 1 year for controlled hypertension or certain other conditions. Some conditions require monitoring every 90 days.

You must maintain a current DOT medical certificate at all times while driving commercially. If it expires, you cannot legally drive until you renew it.

Tips to Pass Your DOT Physical

  • Get enough sleep the night before — Fatigue temporarily elevates blood pressure
  • Avoid caffeine 2–3 hours before — Coffee raises blood pressure temporarily
  • Bring your glasses or contacts if you need them — Vision is tested with correction if you use it
  • Bring a list of your medications — The examiner needs to review all current prescriptions
  • Bring your glasses prescription — Some examiners request it
  • Don't skip your blood pressure medication — Take it as prescribed on exam day
