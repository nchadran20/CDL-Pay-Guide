How Much Does CDL School Cost in Tennessee? (2026 Real Prices)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Tennessee is one of the most affordable states in the country for CDL training. Combined with no state income tax and a strong freight market centered around Nashville and Memphis, Tennessee offers one of the best overall value propositions for anyone starting a trucking career.

📊 Tennessee CDL School Cost Summary

Private CDL schools: $3,000–$7,500. Tennessee Colleges of Applied Technology (TCATs): $1,800–$3,500. Company-paid programs: $0 upfront. State licensing fees: approximately $82.

Tennessee Colleges of Applied Technology (TCATs)

Tennessee's state technical college system offers some of the lowest CDL tuition prices in the country. TCATs are located statewide including Nashville, Memphis, Knoxville, Chattanooga, and smaller cities. These programs are state-subsidized and FMCSA-approved. For Tennessee residents, TCATs are almost always the best value option.

Financial Aid for Tennessee CDL Students

  • Tennessee Promise / Tennessee Reconnect — Some TCAT CDL programs qualify for these state-funded tuition programs
  • Tennessee Department of Labor — Workforce Programs — CDL training funding for eligible unemployed or underemployed workers
  • GI Bill / Tennessee VALOR Act — Veterans can use education benefits at approved CDL programs
  • WIOA Title I — Available through Tennessee American Job Centers

Is CDL School Worth It in Tennessee?

Tennessee CDL drivers earn $54,200/year on average — with zero state income tax, so every dollar stays in your pocket. At $3,000 for TCAT tuition, payback takes roughly 4 weeks of working. Tennessee is one of the best ROI states for CDL school in the entire country.
