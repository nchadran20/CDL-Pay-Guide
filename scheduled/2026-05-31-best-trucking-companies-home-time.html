Best Trucking Companies for Home Time in 2026

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

Home time is the most common reason drivers change carriers — and the most common thing carriers lie about in job postings. "Home weekly" can mean anything from Friday night to Sunday morning to "once a week whether you need it or not." Here's the honest ranking of carriers that consistently deliver on their home time promises in 2026.

Home Time Reality Check

There are three types of home time: guaranteed (it's in your contract), policy (they try but freight sometimes prevents it), and advertised (what they say in job postings that rarely matches reality). Always ask specifically: "Is home time guaranteed or best-effort?" The answer tells you everything.

Best Carriers for Home Time — Ranked by Reliability

1. J.B. Hunt Intermodal (JBI) — Home Daily to Weekly

J.B. Hunt's intermodal division is the gold standard for home time in large-carrier trucking. Drivers move containers between rail yards and local destinations on routes typically 50–250 miles. Most JBI drivers are home daily or at most 2–3 nights away per week. Pay is slightly less than OTR but the quality-of-life difference is significant.

  • Home time: Daily or near-daily for most routes
  • Pay: $65,000–$80,000/year
  • Home time type: Structural — built into the job design

2. Schneider Intermodal — Home Nights and Weekends

Schneider's intermodal division offers similar home time to JBI — local container moves with nightly home time for most positions. Schneider also has dedicated accounts with strong home time schedules.

  • Home time: Daily or near-daily
  • Pay: $60,000–$75,000/year
  • Home time type: Structural

3. Old Dominion Freight Line — Home Nightly (LTL)

Old Dominion is consistently ranked as one of the best LTL carriers in the US. Linehaul drivers are home multiple times per week. Terminal drivers are home nightly. Pay is competitive and the company culture is well-regarded.

  • Home time: Nightly for terminal drivers; 3–4 nights home/week for linehaul
  • Pay: $65,000–$85,000/year
  • Home time type: Structural (LTL route design)

4. Walmart Private Fleet — Home Weekly with Consistency

Walmart's private fleet is one of the most sought-after driving jobs in the country — $90,000–$110,000/year with home time every week. The catch: they typically require 30 months of experience and a spotless record. It's a goal to work toward, not a first job.

  • Home time: Guaranteed weekly
  • Pay: $90,000–$110,000/year
  • Home time type: Guaranteed in contract

5. Werner Regional Division — Home Weekends

Werner's regional division consistently delivers weekend home time for most drivers. Not every weekend is Friday night to Monday morning, but the home time is generally reliable compared to other regional programs.

  • Home time: Most weekends
  • Pay: $62,000–$76,000/year
  • Home time type: Policy-based (reliable but not guaranteed)

6. Amazon Linehaul — Home Nightly

Amazon's linehaul drivers run routes between fulfillment centers and delivery stations — typically 150–350 mile runs with predictable schedules. Most are home nightly or by early morning. Pay is competitive and schedules are consistent.

  • Home time: Nightly for most positions
  • Pay: $65,000–$82,000/year
  • Home time type: Structural

Red Flags in Home Time Advertising

  • "Home weekly" — Ask exactly which day and for how long. "Home weekly" could mean 34 hours every 10–14 days.
  • "Home most weekends" — "Most" is doing a lot of work in that sentence. Ask for the percentage.
  • "Regional routes" — Regional doesn't automatically mean good home time. Ask specifically.
  • Any number above 500 miles from home — If your routes regularly go 800+ miles, you're not getting home consistently regardless of what they promise.
