How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Pennsylvania? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Pennsylvania — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Pennsylvania by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Pennsylvania Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Pennsylvania: $59,200/year. Pennsylvania has a flat state income tax rate of 3.07% — one of the lower rates among states with income tax. Pennsylvania sits on the I-78, I-80, and I-76 (Pennsylvania Turnpike) corridors connecting the Northeast to the Midwest. Major freight state with steel, manufacturing, and pharmaceutical industries.

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Pennsylvania Truck Driver

  • The Lehigh Valley is booming — Allentown and Bethlehem have become one of the hottest warehouse markets on the East Coast. Amazon, Target, and dozens of others have massive facilities there driving strong driver demand.
  • Pennsylvania Turnpike expertise is valuable — Drivers experienced with PA's mountain grades and winter conditions are in high demand for year-round OTR work.
  • Low income tax is an advantage — Pennsylvania's 3.07% flat rate is significantly lower than neighboring New York and New Jersey.
  • Philadelphia port access — Port of Philadelphia drayage work is available for local drivers seeking premium local pay.

CDL Requirements in Pennsylvania

To drive commercially in Pennsylvania you need a valid Pennsylvania CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Pennsylvania) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Pennsylvania driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
