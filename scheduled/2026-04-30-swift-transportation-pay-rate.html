Swift Transportation Pay Rate 2026: What Drivers Actually Earn

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Swift Transportation is the nation's largest truckload carrier — and one of the most common starting points for new CDL drivers. Here's exactly what Swift pays in 2026, including starting rates, annual earnings, and what drivers say about working there.

Swift 2026 Pay Summary

Starting pay: $0.50–$0.58/mile. Average first-year earnings: $52,000–$62,000. Sign-on bonus: up to $8,000 for experienced drivers. Company-paid CDL training through Swift Academy.

Swift Academy — Free CDL Training

Swift operates Swift Academy for new drivers — one of the largest company-run CDL training programs in the US. Key details:

  • 100% company-paid tuition
  • 1-year driving commitment after graduation
  • Training locations in multiple states
  • Orientation pay provided during training
  • Guaranteed job with Swift upon graduation

Swift Benefits Package

  • Medical, dental, and vision (available after 30 days)
  • 401(k) with company match
  • Paid time off
  • Weekly pay via direct deposit
  • Rider and pet policies
  • Newer Peterbilt and Freightliner equipment

What Swift Drivers Actually Say

Swift's reviews are mixed — typical for a large OTR carrier. Common positives: guaranteed freight (you rarely sit), nationwide terminal network, and Swift Academy providing a genuine path for new drivers. Common negatives: dispatcher inconsistency depending on terminal, typical OTR away-from-home challenges, and some drivers reporting miles below expectations in slow periods.

Swift is best viewed as a starting carrier — a place to build 1–2 years of experience before potentially moving to a better-paying regional or dedicated position.
