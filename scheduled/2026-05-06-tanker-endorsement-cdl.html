CDL Tanker Endorsement: How to Get It and What It Pays (2026)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

The Tanker endorsement is one of the easiest CDL add-ons to get — just one written test, no extra background check, and no additional driving test. Yet it opens the door to some of the highest-paying CDL jobs in the country. Here's the complete breakdown.

🔋 Tanker Fast Facts

Pay increase over standard dry van: $8,000–$25,000/year. Requirements: 1 written test at DMV. Extra background check: No. Additional driving test: No. Time to add to CDL: 1 DMV visit. Worth it: Absolutely.

What the Tanker Endorsement Covers

The Tanker endorsement (coded "N" on your CDL) is required to operate any commercial motor vehicle designed to transport liquid or gaseous materials in a tank or tanks that are either permanently or temporarily attached to the vehicle. This includes:

  • Fuel tankers (gasoline, diesel, jet fuel)
  • Milk and liquid food tankers
  • Chemical tankers
  • Water tankers
  • Liquid natural gas and compressed gas tankers
  • Any portable tank with a capacity of 1,000 gallons or more

Note: Dry bulk tankers (cement, grain, dry chemicals) generally do NOT require a tanker endorsement as they don't haul liquids. Check with your employer to confirm requirements for specific cargo types.

How to Get the Tanker Endorsement

  1. Study the tanker section of your state CDL manual — covers liquid surge and its effect on vehicle handling, loading and unloading requirements, and safety rules specific to tanker operations.
  2. Pass the Tanker written test at your state DMV — typically 20–30 multiple choice questions. Most drivers pass on the first attempt with 1–3 hours of study.
  3. Pay the state endorsement fee — usually $10–$40 depending on state.

That's it. No additional driving test, no background check, no waiting period. You walk out of the DMV the same day with the N endorsement on your CDL.

Tanker vs Dry Van — Is the Switch Worth It?

For most drivers, adding a tanker endorsement and transitioning to tanker work is one of the highest-return career moves available. The pay premium is real, the endorsement is easy to get, and demand for tanker drivers consistently outpaces supply. The main challenges of tanker driving vs dry van:

  • Liquid surge — Partially filled tanks slosh during acceleration and braking. Takes experience to manage safely.
  • Loading/unloading responsibility — Tanker drivers often manage their own loading and unloading operations
  • Stricter safety protocols — Higher consequences for accidents mean more pressure on safe operation
  • Physical demands — Some tanker operations involve hose connections, valve operation, and more hands-on work than drop-and-hook

Combining Tanker with Hazmat — The X Endorsement

If you add both the Tanker (N) and Hazmat (H) endorsements, they automatically combine into the X endorsement on your CDL. The X endorsement is the most valuable combination in the CDL world — required for fuel hauling, chemical transport, and oil field tanker work.
