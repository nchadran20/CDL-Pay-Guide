How Old Do You Have to Be to Get a CDL? (2026 Age Requirements by State)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

The short answer: 18 for driving within your state, 21 for crossing state lines. But there's more to it than that — including a new federal program that allows 18–20 year olds to drive interstate commercially under specific conditions. Here's the complete 2026 breakdown.

📋 CDL Age Requirements Summary

18 years old: Can get a CDL and drive commercially within your home state (intrastate). 21 years old: Can drive across state lines (interstate) — required for most trucking jobs. 18–20 with EPAP: Can now drive interstate under the new federal apprenticeship program.

The Basic Age Rule

Federal regulations set the age requirements for CDL holders:

  • 18 years old minimum to obtain a CDL and drive commercially within your state (intrastate commerce)
  • 21 years old minimum to drive commercially across state lines (interstate commerce) — this applies to all standard trucking jobs

The 21+ requirement for interstate driving is the reason most trucking companies won't hire you until you're 21, even if you already have a CDL. The vast majority of trucking work — OTR, regional, and most local deliveries — involves crossing state lines at some point.

The EPAP Program — Under-21 Interstate Driving

In 2022, the FMCSA launched the Safe Driver Apprenticeship Pilot (SDAP), now formalized as the under-21 CDL program. This allows 18–20 year olds to drive interstate commercially under specific conditions:

  • Must complete an apprenticeship with an approved carrier
  • Requires a probationary phase with an experienced driver present
  • Additional safety requirements beyond standard CDL
  • Limited to carriers that have enrolled in the approved program

This program is still relatively new and not all carriers participate. Check with specific carriers about their participation if you're 18–20 and want to drive interstate.

Starting Your CDL Career at 18

Getting your CDL at 18 is a smart move even if you can't immediately drive interstate. Here's how to use those 2–3 years before you turn 21:

  • Get your CDL through a company-paid program — Some carriers hire 18-year-olds for intrastate work and transition them to interstate routes at 21
  • Drive intrastate locally — Local delivery, construction, agriculture, and many other intrastate CDL jobs don't require crossing state lines
  • Get endorsements — Add Hazmat (at 21+ for interstate), Tanker, Doubles/Triples to maximize your value when you hit 21
  • Build a clean driving record — The most valuable thing you can have at 21 when carriers start competing for you

States With Notable Intrastate Programs for Young Drivers

Some states with large intrastate markets are particularly good for 18–20 year old CDL holders:

  • Texas — Massive intrastate freight market in oil, gas, and agriculture. Many positions don't require crossing state lines.
  • California — Large intrastate agricultural and retail distribution market
  • Florida — Local distribution and port drayage opportunities that stay within the state
