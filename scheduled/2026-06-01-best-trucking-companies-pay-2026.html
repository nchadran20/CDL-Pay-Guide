Best Trucking Companies for Pay in 2026 (Highest Earning Carriers)

Updated: May 2026Read time: 8 min

Not all trucking companies pay the same — and the gap between the lowest and highest paying carriers can be $20,000–$40,000 per year for experienced drivers. Here's who actually pays the most in 2026, based on real driver-reported data rather than advertised rates.

Advertised Rate vs Real Earnings

A carrier advertising $0.65/mile sounds better than one advertising $0.58/mile — but if the high-rate carrier runs 1,800 miles/week and the lower-rate carrier runs 2,500 miles/week, you earn more at the lower rate. Total annual earnings matter more than the per-mile rate. Always ask: "What did your average driver earn last year?"

Highest-Paying Trucking Companies in 2026

1. Walmart Private Fleet — $90,000–$110,000/Year

Walmart's private fleet consistently pays the most of any large-scale trucking operation in the US. Home weekly, consistent routes, excellent benefits, and annual salaries in the $90K–$110K range. Requires 30 months of verifiable tractor-trailer experience and near-perfect MVR. This is the career destination, not the starting point.

2. UPS Freight (Union / Teamsters) — $85,000–$110,000/Year

Teamsters-represented UPS positions offer the highest combination of wage, benefits, and pension of any major carrier. Union contract pay scales exceed non-union equivalents by 25–40%. The catch: getting a Teamsters driving position at UPS requires patience — these jobs are coveted and turnover is low.

3. Amazon Freight — $75,000–$95,000/Year

Amazon has been aggressively growing its freight network and paying above-market to attract experienced drivers. Requires 1+ year of experience. Once you're in, consistent volume, predictable schedules, and above-average compensation make this one of the most sought-after driving jobs in the country.

4. J.B. Hunt Dedicated — $72,000–$90,000/Year

J.B. Hunt's dedicated division puts drivers on accounts with specific shippers — consistent routes, consistent pay, and generally better home time than OTR. The best dedicated accounts pay $80,000–$90,000 with weekly home time. Requires 1–2 years of experience.

5. Old Dominion Freight Line — $72,000–$88,000/Year

Old Dominion is one of the best-run LTL carriers in the US and pays accordingly. Strong profits mean they can afford above-market wages, and their union-like culture (without formal union representation) keeps driver turnover low and pay competitive.

6. Sysco / US Foods — $70,000–$88,000/Year

Food distribution for major food service companies pays well and keeps drivers home nightly. The physical demands of food delivery (significant unloading work) are the tradeoff for the local lifestyle and competitive pay.
