How Much Do Truck Drivers Make in Washington? (2026 Salary Guide)

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

If you're considering a CDL career in Washington — or already driving and want to know if you're being paid fairly — this guide gives you the real 2026 numbers. We break down average truck driver salaries in Washington by experience level, driving type, and city.

📊 Washington Fast Facts for CDL Drivers

Average truck driver salary in Washington: $67,060/year. Washington State has no state income tax — one of the strongest combinations of high salary and no tax in the country. Washington is one of the top-paying states for CDL drivers in the US. Port of Seattle and Port of Tacoma are major Pacific Rim freight hubs. Strong tech, agriculture, and aerospace freight sectors.

Top Trucking Companies Hiring in Washington

  • Amazon — Massive logistics operation centered in Seattle, one of the largest CDL employers in the state
  • COSCO / port drayage carriers — Port of Seattle and Tacoma drayage is among the best-paying local CDL work in the country
  • Albertsons — Northwest grocery chain with a strong private fleet
  • Boeing Supply Chain — Aerospace freight and specialized logistics around the Puget Sound
  • Sysco — Food distribution throughout Washington, local routes available

How to Maximize Your Salary as a Washington Truck Driver

  • Washington is the top value state for CDL drivers — High salary plus no state income tax is the strongest combination available anywhere in the US.
  • Port drayage in Seattle and Tacoma is extremely lucrative — Container drivers at the Puget Sound ports earn $75,000–$100,000 and are home nightly.
  • Agricultural reefer freight pays well — Washington's massive apple, hop, and produce industry creates year-round refrigerated freight demand with strong per-mile rates.
  • Amazon Seattle operations pay above average — Being in Amazon's home market means access to some of the better-compensated delivery and linehaul routes.

CDL Requirements in Washington

To drive commercially in Washington you need a valid Washington CDL. Key requirements:

  • Must be 18+ for intrastate (within Washington) driving
  • Must be 21+ for interstate (crossing state lines) driving — federal requirement in all states
  • Valid Washington driver's license and DOT medical certificate
  • Pass written knowledge tests and a skills test
