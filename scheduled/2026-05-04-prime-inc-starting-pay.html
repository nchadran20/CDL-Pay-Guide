Prime Inc. Starting Pay 2026: What Drivers Earn at Prime

Updated: April 2026Read time: 8 min

Prime Inc. is one of the largest privately held trucking companies in the US, known for its refrigerated and flatbed operations and one of the most active new-driver mentorship programs in the industry. Here's the full 2026 pay breakdown.

Prime Inc. 2026 Pay Summary

Starting pay (new driver): $0.46–$0.54/mile. Experienced refrigerated drivers: $65,000–$78,000/year. Flatbed division: $65,000–$80,000/year. Free CDL training program available through Prime.

Prime's Free CDL Training Program

Prime operates its own CDL training program — Prime School — for new drivers:

  • 100% tuition paid by Prime
  • 1-year driving commitment after graduation
  • Training in Springfield, MO (Prime's home base)
  • Mentorship-focused — strong trainer/trainee culture
  • Graduates enter Prime's refrigerated division with guaranteed work

Prime's Mentorship Program

Prime is specifically known for its mentorship-focused approach to new drivers. After graduating from Prime School, new drivers spend time with an experienced trainer on actual freight runs before going solo. This longer mentorship period (compared to some carriers) results in better-prepared solo drivers and lower accident rates. Many drivers cite Prime's trainer quality as a key differentiator.

Important Note on Lease Purchase

Prime actively promotes lease-purchase truck programs. Experienced trucking industry professionals widely advise against lease-purchase arrangements at any carrier — the effective cost and terms typically do not favor the driver. If you drive for Prime, drive as a company driver and avoid the lease program.

What Prime Drivers Say

Prime earns consistently above-average reviews for a large OTR carrier. Positives: strong mentorship, consistent refrigerated freight, good equipment maintenance, and a sense of company culture for a large carrier. Negatives: starting pay is on the lower end, and some drivers report the mentorship period extends longer than expected before solo miles begin.
